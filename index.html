Our Story

Why We Built Affix

We spent 15 years implementing enterprise software — ERP rollouts, system integrations, data migrations. And every project started the same way: someone in a corner office asking, "Can't we just get a simple report?"

The data was always there — locked in an AS400 that had been humming since the Clinton administration, buried in DB2 tables with field names only one retired developer understood, exported to Excel every Monday morning by someone who really should have been doing something more valuable.

We watched companies spend millions on rip-and-replace projects that took years and broke things that worked. We watched analysts spend days recreating reports that should have taken seconds. We watched executives make gut decisions because the data they needed was technically available but practically unreachable.

But the thing that really kept us up at night? The problems nobody knew about. The margin leak that had been compounding for two quarters. The customer segment that was quietly walking away. The supplier pattern that predicted a shortage three months out. The data had all the signals — but nobody was looking, because nobody knew to look.

Affix exists because we believe two things: First, the answer to "Can I get a report from our legacy system?" should be "Yes. Right now. In English." Second, you shouldn't have to ask at all — the most critical insights should find YOU.

We didn't build Affix for companies that want to throw away their legacy systems. We built it for companies that know those systems contain decades of business intelligence — and deserve both a way to access it and an AI that's smart enough to surface what matters before anyone thinks to ask.
